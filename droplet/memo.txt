Ever tried connecting raindrops on a window as a kid?

imm.rain embodies the Japanese idea of wabisabi (subdued refinement) in a simple game to help pass time while giving you something to think about after every round.

Use your finger or tilt your iPhone to move the droplets around the screen to form a single raindrop. Once all the droplets have gathered into one, the frosted glass will clear, revealing the scenery and a quote. A new game will start after the raindrop has trickled off the screen.

Realistic appearances and natural movements create a calm, relaxing atmosphere. You can even clear your mind by just gazing at the droplets slowly trickling off the screen.

Take a break and forget about the worries of your everyday life by immersing yourself in the soothing world of imm.rain.

Presented by Satoshi Nakajima and ZEPPELIN Inc.

Imm.rain is a new category of application, which offers an altermate immersive experience enabled by the power of iOS7 and OpenGL.

You can interact with imm.rain by touching the screen or tilt the device but it is not a game with a specific goal or scoring system. It's more like an interactive art.

You can run it as a screen saver, when it's on the charging station, or interactive with it by touching the screen or tilting the device - in order to kill time, entertain yourself or meditate.

It's all up to you. Enjoy!
